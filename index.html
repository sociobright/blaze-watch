T CrB sits just off the Crown's arc — about a finger-width from the faint star ε, roughly 5° from Alphecca. Same orientation as the whole-sky view, computed live for your time and place. When it blazes it will roughly match Alphecca — a second bright jewel in the Crown.

  1. Find the Big Dipper and follow its handle's curve — "arc to Arcturus," the bright orange star.
  2. Carry on from Arcturus through faint Izar to Alphecca, the bright star in the little backward-C. That C is the Crown.
  3. The pulsing gold ring marks the exact spot the new star appears — beside the Crown, next to Alphecca. Tap 🔍 Close-up above the map to zoom right in.

From the other side, brilliant blue-white Vega points back to the same place.

When it happens

Four things to do

  • Set the time above to when you're out, face the compass point it names, look nearly straight up.
  • Find the Crown's backward-C and its bright star Alphecca.
  • Look for a new star of about the same brightness right beside it — that's the one.
  • Photograph it — and a "before" shot of the empty Crown makes a great comparison.
